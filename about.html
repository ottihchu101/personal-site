My Story

Aspiring Software Engineer • Focused on Impactful Products

Born in Dallas but raised in Nigeria, I spent six years in boarding school in Lagos before heading to the UK for A-levels. That's where I first tried Python, and it was rough. The basics didn't click right away, and I struggled a lot at the start. But sticking with it changed everything. That frustration turned into real curiosity, and soon I was digging into more languages, frameworks, design tools, and ways to build things that actually work well.

Now, as a Computer Science major with an Economics minor at Emory University, I'm focused on creating practical, user-centered solutions that solve real problems and deliver clear value. I build with tools like HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Python, Node.js, and others, always aiming for clean, efficient code that prioritizes outcomes over show. I keep up with what's happening in the field too, especially how AI is changing development, making workflows smarter and more adaptive.

Outside of tech, I'm really interested in how bigger systems function, from macroeconomics and policy to global patterns. For energy, I turn to basketball, soccer, fashion, culture, and travel. This portfolio shows more than just projects. It reflects how I approach things: structured, straightforward, focused on results, and always looking for the next real impact.
